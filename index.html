Invisible layers

Invisible layers reveal nothing new about the artwork. All the informations are already there, but are invisible to our eye. Analytical tools such as Xray, IR and UV reveal the history of the piece. Image analysis, on the other hand, reveal more about colors, their distribution and dominance.

Xray

Under X-rays illumination damages or alterations to a painting are suddenly revealed even though they are not visible to the naked eye. For example, X-rays show wormholes in a painting. Not only this but also the history of a piece is revealed, the way changes are made reveals when modification happened and viceversa. A throughout interpretation of what x-rays can reveal need to be performed by an heritage conservator.ArtScope can provide insight to conservators but also just stimulate the curiosity of those just watching the piece.

UV light

When UV light shines on a painting, one gets a fluorescence signal back from the materials in the painting. Different types of varnish fluoresce with different shades. For example an old varnish has a stronger greenish fluorescence than a new one. Conservators can also get information about the different pigments. One can for instance see whether a repair of a damage comes from the time at which the piece was originally painted or not. If an damage, for example, have been rectified long after the painting was created, there have been used materials that ages in a different way than the original.

As much as we would love to say: go and get it on the app store, we can't. Artscope is strictly a prototype. The code base is full of hacks, and it works just for one artworks and with the API that was set-up ad-hoc by SMK during the hackaton.

ArtScope was an expoloration of what's possible in hacking our cultural heritage in a week end.

Since you made it so far, you deserve more details. The application for iOs is made with Unity, whereas all the data processing and image analysis is done in python. The data analysis is done beforehand and the results are hardcoded into the unity app. It's not unreasonable to scale this set-up using a client-server approach. Artwork recognition and tracking is set-up just for one artwork. To scale up it is smarter to explore beacon technology instead of straight image recognition because it's faster and more reliable.
